Tulsi Ramsay, The Maker Of Iconic Horror Films & Shows In India, Passes Away At 77 Tulsi Ramsay, The Maker Of Iconic Horror Films & Shows In India, Passes Away At 77 The man who changed the entire game of the horror genre in India has passed away at the age of 77. Tulsi Ramsay, known for directing cult horror TV series, 'Zee Horror Show' has made some of the most iconic horror films like "Veerana", "Purani Haveli" and "Bandh Dawaza". The filmmaker died in a city hospital in Mumbai. "He complained of chest pain and his son Deepak took him to Kokilaben Hospital. He was declared dead at the hospital. He passed away at about 2.30 am," a family member of Ramsay's told PTI. For the longest time in Indian cinema, the Ramsay Brothers ruled the horror genre by making low budget cult movies, which were widely popular for unique mix of horror and erotica. Tulsi Ramsay was the son of F U Ramsay and was one of the seven brothers. He also directed films like "Do Gaz Zameen Ke Niche", "Hotel", "Purana Mandir", and the popular '90s TV series, "Zee Horror Show".
