After Filing Rape Case Against Alok Nath, Vinta Nanda Says Onus Is On Her To Prove Her Story After Filing Rape Case Against Alok Nath, Vinta Nanda Says Onus Is On Her To Prove Her Story When the #MeToo movement had hit India, particularly Bollywood, a lot of people asked why these women are speaking up now – after so many years. It obviously takes a lot of courage to come out, name and shame the sexual predators, especially when the society you live in is still fighting with misogyny and gender inequality. Another reason why it took so much time is the aftermath of speaking up. Writer-producer Vinta Nanda was one amongst those who shared their stories on social media. She didn’t name the predator; she hinted he is the “most Sanskari man” in Bollywood. That was enough for people to comprehend who she was talking about -- Alok Nath, with whom she worked on the show called Tara, at that time. She took her fight forward by filing an FIR against him. Now, that a rape case has been registered, she says that the onus is back on her. “I had filed a police complaint. It took three weeks for them to call me back and say yes we will now file an FIR, which happened last week. Following which, Alok Nath was booked under Indian Penal Code's Section 376 (rape).
