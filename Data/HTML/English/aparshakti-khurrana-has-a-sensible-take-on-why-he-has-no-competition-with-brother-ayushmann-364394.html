When siblings enter a similar profession, there are several questions around competition and rivalry. For actors Ayushmann Khurrana and brother Aparshakti Khurrana, there is no scope of competition between them. Talking to IANS, Aparshakti said, Twitter “Competition generally happens between two equals. In our case, Ayushmann and I are in different spaces of cinema. He has always been a front-runner. His films differ from mine, and so does our audience”. He justified how there can't be any competition between two equals. He added, Twitter “There can’t be any comparison, because comparison takes place between two equals and we both have different paths in the industry. There can’t be any competition." He also shared his desire to work with his older brother on screen. The actor who was last seen in Luka Chuppi ( 2019)and Stree (2018), has been appreciated for his nuanced comical performances. I feel there is a need for such characters in our films. The film does not solely work on a single hero. It takes proper teamwork and coordination to make a film hit,” he said. He further added, “If filmmakers feel I am capable of such roles, there is nothing about being typecast. It all depends upon relevant roles and scripts." Not many know that Aparshakti was a practicing lawyer before getting into anchoring for radio shows and then acting.
