Pankaj Tripathi Explains How Science Makes Life Easier But Art Makes It Worth Living

Authors : No Author Specified

Published on : 16/04/2019, 18:32:32

“In life, we have two segments: arts and science. We need science to make our lives easier. But what do we live for? We enjoy life through arts. So it’s an artistes duty to question the wrongs happening in the society. “To make a satire out of it. To make people aware through their art forms — writing, painting, acting, etc” Tripathi told PTI.

Pankaj Tripathi is one actor who is not just loved for his acting calibre, but people love his sincerity and humility as well. In his recent interview, Pankaj Tripathi expressed how he believes a doctor can save a dying man, but an artiste can do a lot more: heal a dying society by questioning the wrong and upholding the right. The actor says an artist’s work should never exist in vanity as while science makes life easier, arts give it a meaning. “In life, we have two segments: arts and science. We need science to make our lives easier. But what do we live for? We enjoy life through arts. So it’s an artistes duty to question the wrongs happening in the society. “To make a satire out of it. To make people aware through their art forms — writing, painting, acting, etc” Tripathi told PTI. They should be aware of what’s happening in the society and make a commentary. They’re not in a la la land. “Today we have films made on several topics. To make sure an actor never loses his edge, Tripathi says it is of utmost importance for one to be aware about the surroundings. Twitter “An actor must read a lot. Books not only make one a good actor, but also a better human being. Second, you must work on your senses. We don’t realise but we are losing the sense of sound slowly. When was the last time you registered listening to a flowing river or a chirping bird? “We are losing the essence of touch. An actor has to work on his senses. You just have to be present for this, be aware. In life, we create. In cinema, actors recreate and for this, your mind and body need to be in sync.” (With agency inputs)
