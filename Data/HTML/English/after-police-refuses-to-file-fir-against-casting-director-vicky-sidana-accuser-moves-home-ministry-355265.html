After Police Refuses To File FIR Against Casting Director Vicky Sidana, Accuser Moves Home Ministry

Authors : Isha Sharma,

Published on : 23/10/2018, 11:44:58

The actress had approached Versova Police station on October 18, 2018 to file a complaint against casting director Vicky Sidana of sexual harassment but after the police failed, she has now approached has now approached the Ministry of Home Affairs, Government of Maharashtra.

On 18th October 2018, actress Kritika Sharma spoke about her #MeToo story and accused casting director Vicky Sidana of sexual misconduct. Kritika and her lawyer visited the Versova Police Station to file an official complaint. But reports claim how the senior inspector at the Versova Police station, Ravindra Badgujar refused to file a complaint. The officer added that they will have to investigate before filing an FIR since the incident is 5 years old. Agencies In an interview with a leading daily, she had earlier stated that Mrs. CG Patil, the female officer at Versova Police station, who recorded her statement was cooperative and did in fact, report her statement over a period of 4 hours. She added that she was promised that an FIR would be filed but later, senior inspector refused on the grounds of the incident being 5 years old. The actor has decided not to give up and she decided to continue her battle, for which she moved to the Ministry of Home Affairs, Government of Maharashtra.
