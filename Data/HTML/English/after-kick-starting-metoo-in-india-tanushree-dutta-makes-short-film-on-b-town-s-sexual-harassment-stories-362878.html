Tanushree Dutta’s career in Bollywood might have been short-lived, but what she did last year will mark history. The actress who had spoken about it 10 years ago as well, once again claimed that she was sexually harassed by Nana Patekar on the sets of a film, but since nobody believed her story and her voice was curbed, she had to quit the industry. For the first time, somebody had named and shamed the offender, and that indeed made waves across the nation. So much so, that more women gained the courage to speak about their sexual harassment stories and what they had to go through. This kick-started #MeToo movement in Bollywood. Now, Tanushree Dutta is bringing Bollywood’s sexual harassment stories to screen with her upcoming short film titled Inspiration, which will be released on March 8 to mark International Women’s Day, claim reports. The movie will be released online. It will deal with the issue of “wilful exploitation of young newcomers in showbiz” apart from other fields. Tanushree Dutta said it is going to be new “reality meets fiction” format, she told Mid Day.
