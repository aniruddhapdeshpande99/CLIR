Whether we talk about it or not, we all know that sexual desire in women is normal. But that's not what we show in our Hindi films Ironically, in one of the world's most populated countries, talking about sex is taboo. Add to that the fact that misogyny is still raging in India. All this has taken a toll on how sex is portrayed on the big screen. In one word: unrealistic. Twitter According to Zoya Akhtar, for the longest time, the industry showed physical abuse on the big screen instead of consensual sex. She thinks that it is problematic especially when people are consuming such content at a young age. Zoya Akhtar says that we might not realise it now, but the impact is visible later. “I realised this much later that while I was growing up, I have only seen physical abuse in Hindi films. It was crazy because we were allowed to watch rape scenes, molestation and assault but we weren't allowed to see consensual sex,” she was quoted as saying by PTI.
