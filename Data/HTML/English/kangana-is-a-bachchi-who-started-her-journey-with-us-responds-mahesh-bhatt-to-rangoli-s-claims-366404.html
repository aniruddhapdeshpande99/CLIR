Things have been sour between the Bhatt family and sisters Kangana and Rangoli Chandel. The duo has time and again targeted and accused the Bhatt family. From Soni Razdan, Alia Bhatt to Mahesh Bhatt, Kangana and Rangoli have called all of them out for different reasons. While Alia and Soni Razdan chose to maintain a dignified silence, Mahesh Bhatt had something to different to say. His comments came after Rangoli attacked him and his wife Soni Razdan earlier this month. "...but later when she went for Woh Lamhe preview to a theatre, he threw chappal at her, he didn't allow her to see her own film, she cried the whole night .... and she was just 19 years old (sic)," she had tweeted. Rangoli also attacked Bhatt's wife and their superstar daughter Alia Bhatt and called them "non-Indian". She further accused Bhatt of 'throwing' a chappal at her at a film screening. "Media have taken filmy kids love too far. Stop pampering mediocre work or else bar will never be raised," the Manikarnika actress had said. Calling Kangana Ranaut a "bachchi", filmmaker Mahesh Bhatt has said he would not like to comment against a "bachchi" (child), who started her Bollywood journey as an actress from his production company Vishesh Films. On being asked about the recent social media attack by Kangana's sister Rangoli Chandel, Bhatt told reporters, Twitter "...she (Kangana) is a 'bachchi', started her journey with us. Just because her relative (sister Rangoli) is attacking me, I won't comment." He added, "Our upbringing and culture teach us that we should not raise a finger on our children. So saying anything against our children won't be possible. My upbringing stops me to do so." Bhatt was talking to media-persons at the screening of the film Yours Truly, which marks his acting debut. "Till I die, I will never ever say anything against our child because it is against my upbringing, it is against my nature," he maintained. Just to jog your memory a little, Kangana started her acting journey in Bollywood in 2006 with the film Gangster, which was produced by Mahesh Bhatt. (With agency inputs)
