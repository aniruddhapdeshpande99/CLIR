One of the most significant and important changes that we saw in the Indian film industry was how #MeToo movement made its way and wiped off (Almost) all sexual predators. By wiped off, we mean how many were called out and dismissed from their respective powerful positions. Thanks to Tanushree Dutta who chose to share her story and it gave courage to several men and women in India to share their #MeToo stories. One of the actors who had given her piece of mind on the #MeToo movement was Aditi Rao Hydari. In October, she took to Twitter to write, Agencies Slow clap for the dizzying heights of hypocrisy... People who are clearly guilty of major harassment giving gyaan on the #MeTooIndia movement... — Aditi Rao Hydari (@aditiraohydari) October 12, 2018 Without naming or shaming anyone, she slyly posted about people's hypocrisy. The actress once again spoke about the #MeToo campaign at the India Today Conclave South 2018. Addressing the audience, she said, Agencies “I was extra naive when I joined the industry. There was an incident that did not harm me so much, but it still had an impact on me. I had to make a choice, and obviously, for me, the choice was clear. I chose not to submit. I did not find work for eight months. I snapped out of it. Today, I'm working with people I want to work with.”
