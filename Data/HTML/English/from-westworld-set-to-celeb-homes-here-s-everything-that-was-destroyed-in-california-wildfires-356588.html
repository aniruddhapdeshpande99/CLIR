Californians are going through a really hard time. For the uninitiated, thousands of the people living in Southern California lost their homes to the blaze that broke out on Thursday. The Woolsey Fire, has already burned 91,572 acres of land, claimed many lives and left several stranded as an estimated 370 homes as of Monday morning, have been burnt to ashes, according to the California Department of Forestry and Fire Protection. AFP As for Northern California, the Camp Fire near Oroville has torched 111,000 acres, killed 42 people and destroyed 6,453 homes as of Sunday evening, reported Cal Fire. The first responders are battling blazes on both ends of the state popularly called as the Town Of Paradise. At this very moment, three fires - The Woolsey Fire, The Camp Fire and The Hills Fire are blazing across the US state of California. The reason? Dry underbrush, combined with winds and hot temperatures became the perfect breeding ground for fires to ignite. While the exact cause is still unknown, the seasonal Santa Ana winds have been spreading the flames rapidly.
