Ghajini It is a Bollywood remake of Murugadoss's own 2005 Tamil film of the same name, which in turn was inspired by the film Memento (2000). Drishyam The film is a remake of Jeethu Joseph's 2013 Malayalam film Drishyam. When it comes to suspense and thriller, no one does like South films. The movie down south are definitely more realistic and heart touching than the song and dance routine Hindi cinema had earlier seen. Made on a small budget, Drishyam was a breath of fresh air so much so that the film was declared tax free in Uttar Pradesh. 5. Singham This introduced the concept of a masala cop drama with flying cars and dollops of action. Singham is a remake of the 2010 Tamil film Singam featuring Suriya and Anushka Shetty. This movie kick-started the career of Rohit Shetty as it began the Singham franchise. The movie was followed by Singham Returns, Simmba and Sooryavansham 6. Rowdy Rathore It is an official remake of 2006 Telugu film Vikramarkudu featuring Ravi Teja and Anushka Shetty. Starring Sonakshi SInha and Akshay Kumar,the movie is a frame by frame copy of the original film. Having seen both, I can tell the director played safe and did not pull off any innovative scenes or stunts for this one. Maybe which is why, this was declared a blockbuster. 7. Yuva koimoi Directed by Mani Ratnam and released in 2004, the filmmaker tried a spectacular mix of language. He was one of the few directors who shot the Hindi and Tamil version simultaneously. While Aaytha Ezhuthu gained recognition over the years, becoming a classic and a cult film in Tamil cinema, Yuva recieved a mix response among the Hindi audience. 8. Saathiya The only movie Vivek Oberoi is remembered by, apart from the current battle with Modi biopic that is yet to witness the release, is Saathiya. It is a remake of the Tamil film Alaipayuthey whose climax was reported to be inspired by the 1998 movie Sliding Doors. Saathiya was decalred a massive hit, not only sound track, the acting, chemistry ad plot were appreciate by critics as week as audience. Additionally, the film won six Filmfare Awards which was proof enough that Vivek Oberoi and Rani are here to stay until Salman Khan happened. 9. Kick It is an official remake of the 2009 Telugu movie of the same name. Not just that, the screenplay was adapted by Nadiadwala and Chetan Bhagat. The film features Salman Khan, Jacqueline Fernandez, and Randeep Hooda in the lead roles and Nawazuddin Siddiqui portraying the main antagonist of the film. The film was also remade in Tamil as Thillalangadi. The whole point of remaking South Indian into Bollywood is that the choice of the audience is clear. People are moving on from rom-coms and prefer thriller, drama and political related movies. Bollywood is expanding and exploring with their choices. They can now tell the audience is intelligent and is seeking meatier content. And this kind of collaboration between South Indian languages and Hindi is great because it is only getting the country closer. Stories are being heard, irrespective of what language it is being made. Thanks to the filmmakers, we have varied choices in content, artists and storyline and we aren't heavily dependent on the West.
