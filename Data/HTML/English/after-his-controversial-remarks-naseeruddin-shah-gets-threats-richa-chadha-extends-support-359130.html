After Naseeruddin Shah expressed his concerns on the recent cases of mob lynchings, he once again became the soft target of trolls and political activists. Talking about poison and lynchings, he had said that “poison has already spread” and it will now be difficult to contain it. “In many areas we are witnessing that the death of a cow is more significance than that of a police officer… I feel anxious for my children because tomorrow if a mob surrounds them and asks, 'Are you a Hindu or a Muslim?' they will have no answer. It worries me that I don't see the situation improving anytime soon.” His name was once again embroiled in a controversy thereafter. So much so, that just for his statement, he was being called a traitor. He says such words doesn’t affect him but it is definitely not comforting to hear chants of “Shoot the traitor!”, he told The Hindu an interview. The controversy fuelled up so much that the organisers of Ajmer Literature Festival on Friday called off an event, which was to be addressed by Naseeruddin Shah. Protests were being held out by right-wing activist outside the venue. One of them even hurled ink at Naseeruddin Shah’s poster. Because of the protests, the show was called off.
