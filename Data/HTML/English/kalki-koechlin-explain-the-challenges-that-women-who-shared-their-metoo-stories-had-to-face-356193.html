The #MeToo movement in India gained a surprising momentum after Tanushree Dutta stood strong and started a sincere conversation around it. The movement spread like a wildfire on social media and some very shocking names came out after several women shared their horror tales of getting harassed by men in power. It won't be right to say that the movement has fizzled out because the conversation around it is still happening. The latest one to join this conversation is actress Kalki Koechlin. Pinterest According to Kalki, #MeToo movement has been an empowering one for women in India. In a conversation with Cosmopolitan, Kalki said, Pinterest "It's an empowering time for women. It's been about a year since it began in Hollywood, and I don't think anyone, including myself, expected it to blow up so suddenly. And we have seen results.
