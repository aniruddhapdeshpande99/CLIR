There are a very few people who have the courage and the will to follow their own heart and dreams and Priyanka Chopra is surely one of them. She has made her own choices and lived each one of them in the most glorious possible way. But the actress says glorification of trolling has added "crazy pressure" on entertainers. According to PC, increasing prevalence of trolling culture only leads to bullying and depression. Agencies "First of all, the pressure comes from people's opinion and how in today's age everyone's opinion becomes news," Priyanka told IANS in an interview when asked about the pressures that comes with the 'global star' tag. Agencies "Most of the time, I see media writing about trolling that someone got trolled for this and that. I have never understood how somebody's opinion becomes news. How the media gives such credence to maybe 500, 600 or 1000 people writing behind the anonymity of the computer," added the actress, who came to India for professional as well as personal reasons. Priyanka was promoting her Hollywood film Isn't It Romantic, which released in India on streaming platform Netflix when she spoke at length about trolling.She admits there are "pressures of living in the digital world". It is just created by the one thing which is the internet. It has has made people's job easier... You can make a story out of anybody's comment." The Quantico star thinks the "glorification of trolling has added the crazy pressure on to entertainers". Twitter "There is something called living your life according to the social responsibility that you feel as an individual and then there is something else like living your life according to how everybody else wants to you to live. And that is not the world we should live in," said the actress, who has herself faced the wrath on social media for sometimes her dressing style or using firecrackers on her wedding last year or for taking her husband and singer Nick Jonas' name. Priyanka, who has time and again proved her versatility with projects such as Don, Fashion, 7 Khoon Maaf, Barfi!, Mary Kom and Bajirao Mastani in India, pointed out a serious implication coming out of the trolling culture. Agencies "Our children should not be taught that people's opinions are that important...When they get bullied in school or when they get bullied as teenagers because of the comments written on Instagram and it leads them to depression leading them to take harsh decisions in their life. It is being propagated by these kinds of stories where we give so much importance to what we look like on Instagram or what we talk about on Twitter or what our imagery is." According to Priyanka, that is the only pressure which she feels "sometimes is unnecessary but being put on" a public personality. Talking about herself, she said, Agencies "I have been not someone who has lived my life according to somebody else. I have walked to the beat of my own drum but I am very cognizant of somebody's feelings as a public person." On the professional front, the actress is getting great response for Isn't It Romantic, which tells the story of a New York architect Natalie (Rebel Wilson) who works hard to get noticed at her job, but is more likely to be asked to deliver coffee and bagels than to design. Agencies Priyanka plays the role of a yoga ambassador and the film also stars Liam Hemsworth and Adam Devine. (With IANS inputs)
