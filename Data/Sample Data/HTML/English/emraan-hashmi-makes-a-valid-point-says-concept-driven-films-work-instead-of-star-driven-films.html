Emraan Hashmi Makes A Valid Point, Says Concept-Driven Films Work Instead Of Star-Driven Films

Authors : Isha Sharma,

Published on : 04/03/2019, 12:00:34

Emraan Hashmi finds it difficult to be a smart actor in Bollywood, says ‘Stupidity goes a long way here’ Emraan Hashmi says earlier, films were all about escapism. Producers would hire ‘stars’ who would lure the audiences into theatres. Emraan says, if 2018 is anything to go by, where “pure star-driven films” did not work, good concept is the need of the hour.

According to Bollywood actor Emraan Hashmi, there are not many producers who are ready to take risks in Bollywood. He thinks that as an industry, only stupidity dominates, making it harder for actors to be smart. The actor says today there are at least a handful people trying to push the envelope, which was not the case earlier. Talking to PTI, Emraan said, Instagram “It is difficult being a smart actor in Bollywood. Stupidity goes a long way here. The audience is smart but not too many producers and directors take that (risk) and understand that. Now at least we have the new blood coming up with new stuff." Talking about the audience which has become more intelligent, he added, Agencies “Back then, it was just about making escapist films with a star, do what a star is supposed to do and get bums on seats. Right now, the audience wants to see more than that." Just to jog your memory a little Emraan started his career in mid-2000s, gaining fame for featuring in films which were dubbed bold. However, he carefully chose his projects and eventually shed the serial-kisser tag with films such as Shanghai, Ghanchakkar and Ek Thi Daayan among others. However, Emraan says before he turned towards edgier content, he was “stuck” in a zone for a long time. When one thing works, they just stretch that to make them work again and again. Producers see it as safety net, a cash register. Where the audience is expected not to ask questions. He adds, Agencies “Because of the influence of the web, the audience knows what they’re missing out on here. They want lap up any mediocre content. Right now a handful of actors are doing a variety of things. The audience doesn’t want to see a star-driven vehicle. They want to see another dimension of you, not you wearing the same urban clothes in a foreign location with 50 background dancers.” (With agency inputs)
